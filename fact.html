ILLICIT DRUGS

An illicit drug is one that is illegal to have(e.g methamphetamine,heroin),and the non-medical use
of drugs that are legally available such as pain killers and sleeping pills.

using illicit drugs can lead to health problems such as kidney problem etc



  • Risk of overdose

    • 1.chronic disease( heart and liver problems)
    2.blood-borne viruses(infections e.g hepatitis and hiv)
    3.lower levels of social and emotional wellbeing(mental health problems)
    4.also increases suicide

PREVALENCE

While almost aboriginal(the earliest known habitant of a region i.e
it could be applied to different enthnic groups in Nigeria) people do not use illicit drugs,
the most commonly reported illicit drugs used in nigeria people are;

Category Examples Public Health Impact Notes / Risk Factors
Cannabis Marijuana Memory loss, poor academic/work performance, risk of psychosis Most widely used illicit drug in Nigeria
Opioids Codeine, Tramadol, Morphine Addiction, respiratory depression, liver/kidney damage Often misused as painkillers or stimulants
Stimulants Cocaine, Amphetamines, Methamphetamine (“ice”) Heart problems, aggression, hallucinations, dependence Rising meth production in Nigeria has worsened abuse
Hallucinogens LSD, Ecstasy (MDMA) Altered perception, risky behavior, dehydration, seizures More common in urban youth party scenes
Inhalants Glue, Petrol, Paint thinners, Gas Brain damage, sudden death, impaired judgment Mostly among adolescents and street children
Alcohol (misused) Spirits, Beer, Local brews Liver disease, accidents, violence, addiction Though legal, harmful use is a major problem
Tobacco / Nicotine Cigarettes, Snuff, Shisha Lung cancer, heart disease, addiction Gateway drug for many young people

PREVENTION AND EDUCATION

There are a variety of strategies that can be used to prevent the use of illicit drugs
and educate people about the potential harms of using them.
This strategies includes;


1.build community knowledge of drug-related harms
2.restrict the availability of illicit drugs
3.engage communities and stakeholders in the development of prevention programs

TREATMENT AND SUPPORT

Effective approaches for people that are experiencing problem drug use includes;

  • conselling
  • brief intervention (about how they are)
  • peer support(helping them overcome stigmatization)
  • residential rehabilitation
  • medicines or pharmacoyheraphy
